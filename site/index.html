Lineage

Standing on decades of linear programming applied to nutrition — with some deliberate departures.

  • Stigler 1945, The Cost of Subsistence — original LP formulation, 9 nutrients, 77 foods, hand-solved heuristic at \$39.93/yr. No cap on per-food quantities. summary
  • Dantzig & Laderman 1947 — simplex method applied to Stigler's data; 9 clerks × 120 man-days on desk calculators got \$39.69 (Stigler was off by \$0.24). First large-scale LP solution in history.
  • Garille & Gass 2001, Stigler's Diet Problem Revisited (Operations Research) — revisit with 1990s prices + RDAs, adds palatability constraints. Our structural reference.
  • EAT–Lancet 2019 / 2025 — multi-objective extension with planetary-boundary constraints (GHG, water, land). commission
  • Frontiers in Nutrition 2018, Review of LP to Optimize Diets — canonical survey of cost/nutrition/environment/culture formulations.
  • Full 20-source corpus + per-paper summaries: sources/

Linear program: minimize daily $ subject to RDAs/ULs (NIH ODS, adult M 31–50), per-food serving caps, and dietary-mode exclusions. Solved with HiGHS via scipy.optimize.linprog.

Prices: Kroger Public Products API, refreshed daily at the listed locationIds. Nutrients: USDA FoodData Central. Targets: NIH ODS DRIs.

Limits: Kroger only; promo prices may be loyalty-card-gated; assumes a single adult profile; ignores cooking, waste, bioavailability, and palatability. No per-food serving cap — Stigler-pure LP, so vegan baskets include ~1.5 L of fortified soy milk per day to hit the vitamin D floor from plant food alone.

The food + pills variant adds Kroger-brand multivitamin, B12, and calcium+D3 tablets priced via the same API. Supplement nutrients come from the Supplement Facts label (FDC doesn't cover pills). The cost delta between variants is the supplement arbitrage — typically $1–2/day.
